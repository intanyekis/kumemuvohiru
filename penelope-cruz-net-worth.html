Early Life and Career

Penélope Cruz Sánchez was born in Madrid, Spain in 1974. Her mother was a hairdresser and her father was a mechanic. She has two younger siblings, Mónica and Eduardo, and a half-sister, Salma, from her father’s second marriage. Cruz grew up Catholic and studied ballet at Spain’s National Conservatory for nine years. She also studied theater in New York City for four years.

Cruz decided to pursue acting after seeing Pedro Almodóvar’s “Tie Me Up! Tie Me Down!” in 1990. She was initially rejected by agent Katrina Bayonas due to her young age, but Bayonas eventually signed her. Cruz began appearing in music videos as a dancer, starting with Mecano’s “La Fuerza del Destino” in 1989.

Penélope Cruz’s Career Highlights
