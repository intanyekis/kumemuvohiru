American writer, producer, director and actor Trey Parker has made millions from his adult animated sitcom South Park which he created with his creative partner Matt Stone. Parker has also tried acting and doing films outside his creations. Though he was involved in the technical aspect of some of the movies on the list, he was mainly acting in the list below. Listed below are the top eighth movies which has grossed in the box office both domestically and internationally.

Terror Firmer

With over $1,434 box office, Terror Firmer which was released on October 29, 1999 lands on the eighth spot of the list. The horror movie produced by Troma Entertainment is about the ode to overkill the films which features the blind director. He is an insane hermaphrodite and a nude fat man who is on the run. Carnage ensued when the serial killer appeared but it is not part of the script.

Orgazmo

With over $582,024 domestic gross and $627,287 worldwide box office, Orgazmo which was released on October 23, 1998 lands on the seventh spot of the list. The drama movie produced by Avenging Conscience is about Joe Young played by Trey Parker who is a Mormon who is currently serving his mission in Los Angeles while needing money to fund his wedding with his girlfriend Lisa who was played by Robyn Lynne. With needing the money, he was able to find a job where he was casts as a masked superhero named Orgazmo which was a porn film.

The Aristocrats

With over $6,377,461 domestic gross and $6,702,604 worldwide box office, The Aristocrats which was released on July 29, 2005 lands on the sixth spot of the list. The documentary produced by Mighty Cheese Productions is about the world's funniest dirty jokes which features about 100 stand up comedians who will be performing and telling the jokes. The jokes will involve a great deal of improvisation on their part which is profane and only the comedians have heard the jokes.

Baseketball

With over $7,042,541 box office, BASEketball which was only released in the U.S. on July 31, 1998 lands on the fifth spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Universal Pictures the slacker friends Joe Cooper played by Trey Parker and Doug Remer played by Matt Stone who are challenged to pick up a basketball game against jocks. The two proposed to play a game which they learned called Baseketball which was the combination of sports basketball and baseball. They are actually just improvising all the rules of the game in order to win against the jocks.

Team America: World Police

With over $32,774,834domestic gross and $50,948,811worldwide box office, Team America: World Police which was released on October 15, 2004 lands on the fourth spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Braniff Entertainment is about the North Korean leader Kim Jong Il played by Trey Parker who orchestrates a global terrorism plot and it is up to the Team America’s unit to stop the scheme of the North Korean Leader.

South Park: Bigger, Longer and Uncut

With over $52,037,603 box office, South Park: Bigger Longer & Uncut which was only released in the U.S. on June 30, 1999 lands on the third spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Paramount Pictures, Warner Bros., Comedy Central Films, Scott Rudin Productions and Braniff Productions is about the animated hit series South Park. The third graders of South Park have sneaked into the ultra-vulgar Canadian film of Terrance played by Matt Stone and Philip played by Trey Parker. The third graders have expanded their vocabularies upon entering the set which left their parents and teachers scandalized.

Bowling for Columbine

With over $21,576,018 domestic gross and $$58,240,487 worldwide box office, Bowling for Columbine which was released on October 11, 2002 lands on the second spot of the list. The documentary movie produced by United Artists, Alliance Atlantic, Salter Street Films, Dog Eat Dog Films is about the political documentary of the filmmaker Michael Moore which explores the circumstances that lead to the Columbine High School Massacre which happened on 1999. It tackles the proliferation of the guns and the high homicide rate which is currently happening in America.

Despicable Me 3

With over $264,624,300 domestic gross and $1,032,596,894 worldwide box office, Despicable Me 3 which was released on June 30, 2017 lands on the first spot of the list. The comedy movie produced by Universal Pictures and Illumination is about the mischievous minions who are hoping that their master Gru will to his old life of crime after his new boss Anti-Villain League had fired him. However Gru decided to retire and just travel to Freedonia to finally meet his long lost twin brother for the very first time ever.
