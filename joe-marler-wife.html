Joe Marler Wikipedia: - Joe Marler is an English professional rugby union player. Joe Marler was born on 7 July 1990 in Eastbourne, United Kingdom. Marler was educated at Maynards Green Primary School and Heathfield Community College and began playing for Eastbourne Sharks at the age of 11. Marler began his career at Haywards Heath RFC in Sussex. After spending some time on loan at Esher and Worthing, Marler made his debut for the first team in the 2009–2010 season. Marler joined the Harlequins academy in 2008. Marler made his debut for the Harlequins team in May 2011 when they won the EPCR Challenge Cup Final against Stade Français. Marler started in the 2011–12 Premiership Final victory over Leicester Tigers and played a key role in helping Harlequins win their first league championship the following season. Marler led the Harlequins, replacing Chris Robshaw, who was unavailable due to England's World Cup duties. The following season, Marler handed over the Harlequins captaincy to teammate Danny Care.
