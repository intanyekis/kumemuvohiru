A Look on Eva Marcille Early Life and Education

On October 30, 1984, in Los Angeles, California, Eva Marcille was born as Eva Marcille Pigford. In addition to being African-American, her father’s side of the family is Puerto Rican. When Marcille was younger, she attended Marina Del Rey Middle School after Raymond Avenue Elementary School.

Before leaving for Georgia to enroll at Clark Atlanta University, she later attended Washington Preparatory High School. During her time in college, Marcille participated in the third cycle of the reality television competition show “America’s Next Top Model.”

From 2003 to 2018, the show, which was created and hosted by Tyra Banks, showcased up-and-coming models competing against one another in challenges and photo shoots. In 2004, Marcille defeated Yaya DaCosta, a competitor, to become the third winner of the series.

She won a cover story in Elle, a modeling contract with Ford Models, and a contract with CoverGirl cosmetics. Marcille left Clark Atlanta University after winning “America’s Next Top Model” at the age of 19. Marcille’s victory on “America’s Next Top Model” catapulted her into the world of fashion modeling.

She continued to grace magazine covers, including those for Brides Noir, King, IONA, Essence, and Women’s Health and Fitness. Along with modeling for companies like DKNY, Samsung, Apple Bottoms, and Avon, Marcille was also featured in WeTheUrban, In Touch Weekly, and Star Magazine.

In the meanwhile, she took part in numerous runway events, including the Fall 2005 Marc Bouwer, the Fall 2005 Gharani Strok, the Spring 2006 Naqada, and the Fall 2007 Monarchy Collection LA Fashion Week. In November 2006, Marcille formally renounced the surname Pigford and broke up her relationship with her managers, Tyra Banks and Benny Medina.

She then became a signed agent of LA Models. In the years following her triumph on “America’s Next Top Model,” Marcille has made appearances on several TV programs. Several UPN and The CW shows have included her as a guest star, such as the sitcom “Everybody Hates Chris,” the superhero series “Smallville,” and the courtroom thriller “Kevin Hill.”

Marcille appeared in the Tyler Perry sitcom “House of Payne” as Tracie Evans, a recurrent character, from 2007 until 2012. She played Tyra Hamilton, a young mother, in a regular role on the soap opera “The Young and the Restless” from 2008 to 2009.

The sitcom “The Assistants” and the BET romantic comedy series “Let’s Stay Together” both featured Marcille in episodes after that.

Following that, she had appearances on the reality television series Real Husbands of Hollywood and Girlfriend Confidential: LA. Marcille starred in the TV One sitcom “Born Again Virgin” from 2015 to 2016.

After participating in the first season of the VH1 horror reality series “Scared Famous,” she went on to act as a recurrent cast member on Bravo’s reality series “The Real Housewives of Atlanta,” where she was featured from 2018 to 2021.

Among her numerous television appearances, Marcille was a host of “Rip the Runway” and “My Model Looks Better Than Your Model” on BET. In addition, she served as host of “Hair Battle Spectacular,” an Oxygen reality show, during its debut season.

Marcille has also appeared in television films, such as “For the Love of Lockwood” and “The Fright Night Files.” She was cast in a lead role in the television series “All the Queen’s Men” in 2021. The romantic comedy “Premium” and the basketball drama “Crossover,” both released in 2006, featured Marcille in supporting parts.
